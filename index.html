What Profession Do I Wanna Be?

The IT job that interests me the most is Cyber Security, specifically Ethical Hacking. Ethical hackers are hired by companies to test their systems and find weaknesses before real criminals do. Their daily tasks include scanning networks, running penetration tests, and reporting security risks. I like this role because it feels like your the attacker but using that knowledge to protect people and organizations. The constant changes in technology also mean the work never gets boring.

Evaluation of the Job

This career has many positives. Cyber Security is one of the fastest-growing fields, so job opportunities are wide open, and salaries are often high. More importantly, the work has a real impact because it helps protect sensitive information. However, it also comes with challenges. The job can be stressful when dealing with serious security threats, and the skills needed must always stay up to date. Hackers never stop improving, so ethical hackers can‚Äôt stop learning either.

Analysis of Skills

To succeed, an ethical hacker needs knowledge of computer networks, operating systems, programming, and security tools. Certifications like CEH or CompTIA Security+ are valuable, and soft skills like communication are just as important because findings (bugs with in the software) must be explained clearly to non-technical people. Right now, I already have some basic programming skills and problem-solving ability, but I need to learn more about networking, Linux, and real-world security practices.

Conclusion

To become an Ethical Hacker, I need both technical and personal growth. I should focus on learning Python, networking fundamentals, and ethical hacking tools, while also practicing how to explain technical ideas as simple as possible.

Action Plan

My step by step plan is, by the end of this semester, I will finish an online Ethical Hacker fundamentals course. Within six months, I will practice using tools like Linux on a virtrual machine. In one year, I aim to be more fluent at Python language. Along the way, I will connect with online communities and professionals for guidance.
